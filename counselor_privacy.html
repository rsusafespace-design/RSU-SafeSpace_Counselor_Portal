1 Purpose

The SafeSpace Counselor Portal is designed to provide a secure and confidential environment for student counseling and support. This system handles highly sensitive mental health–related information, and all data collected, processed, and stored are managed in strict compliance with the Data Privacy Act of 2012 (Republic Act No. 10173) and Google's Privacy & Security Principles.

Our Commitment: SafeSpace recognizes the high sensitivity of mental health data and strictly enforces data protection at every layer — from authentication to storage. By leveraging Google's secure Firebase platform and adhering to the Philippine Data Privacy Act, SafeSpace ensures that every message, session, and profile is handled with confidentiality, integrity, and respect.

2 Data Privacy Commitment

SafeSpace ensures that all personal and sensitive data are:

  • Collected lawfully and fairly, only for legitimate counseling purposes.
  • Processed with full confidentiality, following institutional and ethical standards.
  • Protected against unauthorized access, alteration, and disclosure.
  • Stored in a secure, cloud-based Firebase database under Google's secure infrastructure.

We uphold the principles of:

  • Transparency – Users are informed about data collection and usage.
  • Accountability – Only authorized counselors and administrators can access data.
  • Data Minimization – Only necessary information is collected and stored.
3 Data Collected (Counselor Side)

SafeSpace collects and stores the following counselor-related data:

  • Counselor credentials (email, name, position, and account ID)
  • Counseling session logs and message content (text and image data)
  • Student profiles and appointment details
  • Basic system logs (login activity, session time, and access paths)

All counseling messages — including mental health–related communications — are treated as Sensitive Personal Information (SPI) as defined by the National Privacy Commission (NPC).

4 Security Measures Implemented

SafeSpace employs the following security mechanisms:

  • Firebase Authentication – Secure login with encrypted credentials managed by Google.
  • TLS Encryption – All communications between client and Firebase are encrypted in transit.
  • Role-Based Access – Only authenticated counselors can access student and message data.
  • Data Isolation – Each counselor session is authenticated; unauthorized users are redirected to login.
  • Session Management – sessionStorage and localStorage are cleared upon logout or user switch.
  • Cloud Data Security – Firebase Realtime Database and Firestore enforce access control rules and comply with ISO/IEC 27018 standards for cloud privacy.
  • Audit Trail Readiness – System logs and message timestamps provide traceability for accountability.
5 Data Sharing and Disclosure

SafeSpace does not share or sell any counseling data to third parties. Disclosure may only occur:

  • With explicit consent from the counselor or client,
  • When required by law or university policy,
  • When necessary to protect the rights, safety, or welfare of the user or institution.

Only minimal and relevant information is disclosed on a "need-to-know" basis.

6 User Rights (RA 10173)

Under the Data Privacy Act of 2012, users have the right to:

  • Access their personal data stored in the system.
  • Correct or update inaccurate information.
  • Withdraw consent for data processing, subject to institutional procedures.
  • Request deletion of data no longer necessary for counseling or documentation.
  • File complaints with the National Privacy Commission (NPC) regarding any data privacy concern.
7 Data Retention and Disposal

Counseling messages and session data are retained only for as long as needed for institutional and therapeutic purposes. Upon account deletion, all personal and counseling data are permanently removed from Firebase following institutional policy and Firebase's secure deletion standards.

8 Accountability and Contact

SafeSpace operates under the supervision of the university's Data Protection Officer (DPO). For privacy-related questions, contact the DPO or system administrator responsible for SafeSpace compliance.

Encryption Policy

Encryption Status: Active

All counseling messages in SafeSpace are protected with multiple layers of encryption to ensure confidentiality and compliance with RA 10173.

Technical Implementation:

  • End-to-End Encryption: All messages are encrypted during transmission using TLS 1.3
  • At-Rest Encryption: Data stored in Firebase is encrypted using AES-256
  • Key Management: Encryption keys are managed by Google's secure infrastructure
  • Access Control: Messages are only accessible to authorized counselors and the specific student involved

Compliance:

Our encryption practices comply with:

  • Republic Act No. 10173 (Data Privacy Act of 2012)
  • ISO/IEC 27001 Information Security Management
  • Firebase Security Rules and Authentication

Access Control Policy

SafeSpace implements strict access control policies to protect sensitive counseling data in compliance with RA 10173.

Authorized Personnel:

  • Authenticated Counselors (Assigned Cases Only)
  • System Administrators (Limited Access for Maintenance)
  • Data Protection Officer (Audit Access Only)

Restricted Access:

  • Unauthorized University Staff
  • External Third Parties
  • Other Counselors (Cross-Case Access)
  • Students (Other Students' Records)

Access Logging:

All access to counseling data is logged and monitored for compliance. The system maintains:

  • Timestamp of access
  • User identity and role
  • Type of data accessed
  • Purpose of access

Note: All access is governed by the principle of least privilege, ensuring users only have access to data necessary for their specific counseling responsibilities.
